Overview

What is hyperbaric oxygen therapy?
A Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy Chamber is a treatment device that increases the body’s oxygen concentration and alleviates hypoxia by inhaling high‑purity oxygen (99.99%) under high‑pressure conditions. By dissolving large amounts of oxygen into the blood, hyperbaric oxygen therapy delivers oxygen throughout the body via the capillaries. This therapy is highly effective at stimulating new capillary growth in the skin and can help with diabetes (including diabetic complications), Buerger’s disease, burns, chronic headaches, dizziness, and numbness in the hands and feet. Typically, the chamber pressure is raised to 2–3 atmospheres absolute (ATA)—equivalent to the pressure at a water depth of 10–20 meters—while inhaling 99.99% oxygen.

Key Benefits of Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy

  • Increased immune function

  • Enhanced oxygen delivery

  • Accelerated wound healing

  • Reduced inflammation

  • Stimulation of new blood vessel formation in areas with impaired circulation

  • Treatment of infections such as chronic bone disease and gas gangrene

  • Support for recovery at skin graft sites

  • Treatment of carbon monoxide and other gas poisoning symptoms

  • Healing of damaged tissues and reduction of swelling after radiation therapy
